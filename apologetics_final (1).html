The spine — say this to anyone
"The universe had a beginning — and beginnings need causes. Whatever caused all time, space, and matter had to exist outside of those things — timeless, spaceless, immensely powerful. (Some people call that nothing.) That sounds exactly like God. So the real question becomes: would God show up? And the most extraordinary claim in all of human history is that He did. One man, in a specific place, at a specific time, claimed to be God in the flesh. (No philosopher, no prophet, no founder of any religion ever said anything like that.) And then He did something no one else has ever done: He rose from the dead — verified by over 500 eyewitnesses, documented by hostile historians, and never once successfully refuted. The resurrection of that man didn't just prove His divinity — it is proof that death itself has been defeated on our behalf. So everything He said carries the weight of God Himself — including affirming the Scriptures. This means the Bible isn't just the most historically verified set of ancient documents in existence. It is the record of God's rescue, written by the people who witnessed it firsthand. It is the record of God's own words made flesh. And it is the record of God's living voice, still speaking. This all leaves one conclusion: The Bible is the Word of God. He exists. And His name is Jesus."
